The Research Process

Independent, Evidence-Based Research

AIREDEX™ scores are the product of multi-layered research conducted by our team, assisted by advanced AI research tools. We draw on a wide range of publicly available information from a wide range of sources, including institutional announcements, academic program data, research publications, industry partnerships, and policy disclosures. This helps us build a comprehensive picture of each school's AI readiness.

We refresh our research on a regular basis to keep our scores relevant. This helps us track new mandates, partnerships, and progress that schools make on an ongoing basis. The best way to stay current on the schools you care about is to subscribe to AIREDEX™ updates -- we'll notify you when a school you're interested in makes progress, and when new schools are added to our ratings.

AIREDEX™ is an independent organization. We are not affiliated with, sponsored by, or endorsed by any college or university.

Scoring Framework

Five pillars of AI readiness

AIREDEX™ uses a proprietary system to evaluate the AI readiness of every college and university we score. This system measures five distinct aspects of each school's approach to AI, which we break into specific 'pillars,' each described below.

Pillar 01
Institutional Commitment

Has institutional leadership made AI a stated priority? Where we find evidence of dedicated AI leadership roles, AI-specific strategic planning, meaningful resource allocation, and AI infrastructure embedded across the entirety of the school, we are able to evaluate the depth of that commitment -- and distinguish genuine investment from a short-term response to trends.

Pillar 02
Curriculum Integration

Curriculum is where AI readiness has its most direct impact on the student -- in many ways on a par with institutional commitment itself. A school should be incorporating AI across disciplines, not just within computer science. We look for AI integration across majors including Business, Law, Public Policy, Humanities, and other fields -- because the AI revolution will reshape every profession, not just the ones that write code.

Pillar 03
Industry Partnerships

Many schools form partnerships with public and private tech companies to advance AI research, develop curriculum, and build shared infrastructure. When we find evidence of these relationships, it sends a strong signal that the institution is actively engaged with the industry -- which can pay real dividends for students in the form of internship pipelines, recruiting access, and exposure to real-world AI work before graduation.

Pillar 04
Research Depth & Access

A university's research output is one of the clearest indicators of how seriously it takes any academic discipline -- and AI is no exception. A school associated with cutting edge AI research signals to the broader industry that AI is taken seriously there -- which can reflect positively on its graduates. We also look for evidence that undergraduates have genuine access to that research environment.

Pillar 05
A(I)ttitude™

Every institution has a stance toward AI, whether they have articulated it or not. Some are leaning in aggressively, integrating AI across academics, operations, and strategy, while others may be just starting to explore AI. Our proprietary metric, A(I)ttitude™, helps capture that posture across multiple data points and our other scored pillars. Our five designations distill a lot of analysis into sentiments that are easy to understand at a glance.

Fair Comparison

We don't compare apples to oranges

Scoring a small liberal arts college against a major research university on the same absolute scale would be unfair, and misleading. A school with 1,800 undergraduates cannot be expected to have the same AI infrastructure as an institution with 50,000 students and a multi-million dollar research budget.

AIREDEX™ classifies each institution we evaluate into one of three cohorts based on undergraduate enrollment size. Students considering a small college with a few thousand undergraduates should have different expectations for AI infrastructure and resources than students looking at a flagship research university enrolling 50,000. Every school is measured against peers of comparable scale, so that prospective students and applicants can better evaluate whether the school they are interested in is genuinely preparing them for a world where AI fluency will impact their career outcomes.

Scoring Scale

What the tiers mean

AIREDEX™ scores each institution on a scale of 0-100. We then combine those scores with our broader analysis to assign a tier designation, helping prospective students quickly understand where a school stands relative to its peers. Tier thresholds are calibrated separately for each of the three AIREDEX™ cohorts -- large research universities, mid-size regionals, and smaller institutions -- to account for the different standards and expectations appropriate to each.

Tier Label What it means
S Elite Leading edge AI readiness. Significant institutional commitment, cross-disciplinary integration, and active industry engagement are all evident, making this institution a leader in its class.
A Exceptional Meaningful AI investment is underway and commitment to AI is evident throughout the institution. While some gaps may appear, this institution is among the leaders in AI readiness.
B Strong AI readiness is evident, with meaningful involvement across multiple departments. Integration may be incomplete, but the trend toward AI readiness is positive, putting this institution on a strong path to AI leadership.
C Good Emerging AI activity is evident, but it lacks scale or material institutional backing. This institution needs to increase its commitment and resources to put it on a path to AI readiness.
D Limited Little evidence of AI activity has been identified at this institution. This may reflect a negative posture toward AI, or inaction due to lack of resources or focus.
