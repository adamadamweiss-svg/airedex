Our Approach

How we score AI readiness
in higher education

AIREDEX uses a proprietary research methodology combining primary and secondary sources to produce independent, evidence-based scores across five pillars of institutional AI preparedness.

The Research Process

Built on evidence, not opinion

Every AIREDEX score is produced by our research team, supported by a custom AI engine that compiles and analyzes data from publicly available sources. Our team reviews course catalogs, institutional websites, press releases, strategic planning documents, and published reports. Where appropriate, we conduct outreach to university staff, faculty, and current and former students to supplement publicly available information.

Each score reflects conditions at the time of research. We disclose the research date on every school profile, and scores are reviewed and updated on a regular cycle. Where data is limited or inconclusive, we score conservatively and flag those areas transparently in the school profile.

AIREDEX is an independent publication. We are not affiliated with, sponsored by, or endorsed by any college or university. No institution can purchase a higher score.

Scoring Framework

Five pillars of AI readiness

Every institution is scored across five pillars. Each pillar measures a distinct dimension of how seriously, and how effectively, a school is preparing students for an AI-driven world. Together, they produce a single composite score on a 100-point scale.

Pillar 01
Institutional Commitment

Has leadership made AI a stated, funded, structural priority? We look at named initiatives, dedicated offices, strategic plans, and resource commitments, signals that AI is a genuine institutional priority, not a marketing talking point.

Pillar 02
Curriculum Integration

Is AI being taught beyond the computer science department? The most meaningful signal is AI reaching into Business, Law, Public Policy, Humanities, and other disciplines, preparing all graduates, not just future engineers.

Pillar 03
Industry Partnerships

Are influential AI companies actively engaged with this institution? We look for current, named, verifiable relationships, joint labs, funded programs, formal curriculum partnerships, not outdated logos on a webpage.

Pillar 04
Research Depth & Access

Does serious AI research exist at this institution, and can undergraduates access it? Graduate programs and research labs only count if undergraduates can participate, through named programs, open labs, or cross-enrollment.

Pillar 05 · Editorial
A(I)ttitude

What is this institution's philosophical stance toward AI? This pillar reflects our analysts' read of institutional culture, from actively enabling AI use across campus, to neutral, to cautious or restrictive. It is the only pillar scored by editorial judgment rather than verifiable criteria, and it is labeled as such on every profile.

Fair Comparison

We don't compare apples to oranges

Scoring a small liberal arts college against a major research university on the same absolute scale would be unfair, and misleading. A school with 1,800 undergraduates cannot be expected to have the same AI infrastructure as an institution with 50,000 students and a billion-dollar research budget.

AIREDEX classifies every institution into one of four categories based on their academic mission and Carnegie Classification designation. Schools are scored against standards appropriate to their category, and tier grades reflect excellence within each institutional type.

This means an S Tier score at Williams College carries the same weight as an S Tier score at MIT, both represent exceptional AI readiness for their institutional context. Direct score comparisons across categories should be made with that context in mind.

Scoring Scale

What the tiers mean

Scores are expressed as a number out of 100 and assigned a tier grade. Tier thresholds are calibrated separately for Research Universities and Undergraduate-Focused Colleges to account for the different standards applied to each.

Tier Label What it means
S Exceptional Leading AI readiness. Structural commitment, cross-disciplinary integration, and active industry engagement are all evident.
A Strong Meaningful AI investment is underway. Some gaps remain but the direction is clear.
B Developing AI readiness is in progress. Early signals are present but integration is incomplete or uneven.
C Emerging Limited visible AI investment. Some activity exists but lacks scale or institutional backing.
D Limited Little evidence of structured AI readiness. May reflect restriction, inaction, or insufficient public information.
Transparency

What we show you

Every school profile includes the research date, a freshness indicator showing how recent our sources are, source citations for verifiable findings, and confidence indicators on each pillar. Where our analysts could not find direct evidence for a criterion, we score conservatively and mark that pillar accordingly.

We believe the credibility of AIREDEX depends on transparency. We do not hide uncertainty behind false precision. A score of 74 is not more reliable than a score of 75, what matters is the pillar breakdown and the evidence behind it.

Scores marked with * on a pillar indicate limited publicly available data. Our team continues to research these areas and updates scores as new information becomes available.
